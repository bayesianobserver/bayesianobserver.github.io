The past few weeknights have been spent solving puzzles from [Martin Gardner’s](http://www.amazon.com/Mathematical-Logic-Puzzles-Dover-Recreational/dp/0486281523/ref=sr_1_1?s=books&ie=UTF8&qid=1343271516&sr=1-1&keywords=martin+gardner) [books](http://www.amazon.com/Entertaining-Mathematical-Puzzles-Martin-Gardner/dp/0486252116/ref=sr_1_4?s=books&ie=UTF8&qid=1343271516&sr=1-4&keywords=martin+gardner), which I really enjoy. I just came across a book by [Peter Winkler](http://www.math.dartmouth.edu/~pw/http://www.math.dartmouth.edu/~pw/), whose name I had heard before in talks by [Muthu](http://www.cs.rutgers.edu/~muthu/). After reading a few of the puzzles on Amazon’s look inside preview, [this book](http://www.amazon.com/Mathematical-Puzzles-Connoisseurs-Peter-Winkler/dp/1568812019/ref=sr_1_3?s=books&ie=UTF8&qid=1343270546&sr=1-3&keywords=puzzles) is too hard to resist buying. Also, [here](http://www.math.dartmouth.edu/~pw/solutions.pdf) [PDF] is a beautiful set of 7 puzzles compiled by Peter Winkler on the occasion of a remembrance for Martin Gardner in 2006.  Other places I have found good puzzles recently include [this page](http://gurmeet.net/puzzles/) maintained by Gurmeet Singh Manku.

Puzzles + coffee + weekend afternoon = awesomeness.
