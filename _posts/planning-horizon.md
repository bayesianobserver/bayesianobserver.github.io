Every person has a planning horizon. For animals, as far as we know, this is realtively short - perhaps the next few seconds. For humans, this horizon varies from hours to years. The source of this variation is of interest. I supect, the factors include: 

- Wealth: those in want, probably fid it hard to plan too far ahead. 
- Intelligence: if everything else is the same, I posit that greater intelligence allows for individuals to plan farther ahead. 

I believe this concept of planning horizon extends to entire societies, for societies are after all collections of individuals. Further, I believe that this planning horizon is manifest in the public investment markets. That is, different individuals have different investment horizons that they care about. Some care about the long term, add money to an investment vehicle, and don't think about it for a long time. some others have horizons more like 6 months to 1 year.
