The goal is not self-improvement (more skills, more wealth, more health, etc.). The goal is better engagement with the world. You can be the world's best at self-improvement, but if you are sitting in a cave, where the rest of the world doesn't have a way of finding you, then your existence and all your wonderful skills do not matter to the world. 

Price is merely a signal in an open market economy that communicates relative imbalance between demand and supply. Hence, a particular job / skill might have a high salary because of a demand-supply imbalance (salary is a price). But keep in mind that price is a fluctuating quantity because demand-supply imbalance can change with time. Price is therefore a difficult thing to optimize over a career lifespan. It is better to optimize for 'quantity and impact of engagement with the world'. This can sometimes translate to the same actions as chasing price, and sometimes to different actions.
