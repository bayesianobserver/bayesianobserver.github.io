# About Me

I am an applied machine learning researcher and electrical engineer working at the intersection of **3D computer vision, geometric learning, generative modeling, and physical AI**.

I currently work at **Dandy**, where I lead projects involving 3D point clouds, dental shape generation, SE(3)-equivariant models, defect detection, shape retrieval, and 2D-to-3D registration for automated design and manufacturing.

Before this, I worked on large-scale machine learning and statistical modeling at **Jain Global, Bloomberg, Two Sigma, and AT&T Labs**. Across these roles, I have focused on a recurring problem: extracting reliable structure from noisy, incomplete, or indirect measurements.

My background is in electrical and computer engineering, signal processing, probability, statistics, and information theory. I hold a Ph.D. WINLAB from Rutgers University and a B.Tech. in Electrical Engineering from IIT Madras.

My current interests include:

* 3D vision and geometric deep learning
* Generative models for structured physical objects
* Inverse problems and uncertainty
* Scientific machine learning
* Reliable evaluation and deployment of ML systems

My work has resulted in **8 U.S. patents** (one licensed to the Ford Motor Company), best-paper awards at ACM MobiSys and NYU CSAW, and publications across wireless systems, network security, information theory, and machine learning.

This website is where I write about machine learning, geometry, sensing, biology, medicine, and scientific inference.
